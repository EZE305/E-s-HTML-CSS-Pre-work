Background

A Story Involving Weed...

Growing up in Miami I had difficulty finding something that I was passionate about. Something that I found interested that also allowed me to make a comfortable living. 3.5 years into a business degree I saw headlines on the news about the "Green Rush" happening in places like Colorado and California. I was strongly driven to learn as much about the newly booming legal cannabis market. I changed my course of study to Agriculture Science and attempted to apply everything I learned to cannabis. I developed technical lab skills working in the Microbiology lab and learned how to grow plants/food while managing the Organic Garden at my university. All this knowledge was directly applied into developing a career in cannabis. This was 6 years ago and through this journey I've grown indoors and outdoors, mixed light and in greenhouses. I realized my degree was being underutilized and switched to working in a lab setting where I learned how to make medical grade extracts. This led me to a company making recreational extracts and with my background I was able to climb up the ladder eventually becoming the lab director for a small company. Over the last 2 years the green rush has drastically died down. Even the biggest brands and largest names are having difficulty making money regardless of the quality of the product. I decided to diversify my skill set and get into a more secure and lucrative industry that would also allow for more autonomy in terms of workspace and salary as cannabis scientists are severely underpaid and underappreciated. I started this coding bootcamp just a month before being let go due to a steady decline in sales. Now I strive to develop my career in tech and through the learning process find exactly where in this industry I best fit in.

Motivation/Passion

Why ask why?

Maturing and gaining perspective has allowed for a re-prioritization of what is important, and what life really is about. Starting 2022 I found myself alone in California after pursuing a dream. Proud of all that I had achieved, but feeling an emptiness brought upon being so far from my family and friends I left in Miami 5 years ago. Never truly feeling at home in the Bay Area, I reflected on the time I was happiest. It was a time I was also the busiest I had ever been, but surrounded by friends and loved ones. This sparked an intense urge to travel as many places as I could. I thankfully had important people by my side which made it all worthwhile. Being able to work from anywhere became a top priority this past year in pursuing a new career along with earning enough to make a decent living.
